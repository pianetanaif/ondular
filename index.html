Layering awareness

A community driven approach to user research

User research, as I see it, it’s about building layers.

Like designing a city that starts with a single house, then a neighborhood, and eventually an entire community that takes on a life of its own.

My approach to user research is a stacked model, where each level depends on the groundwork laid by the previous one, much like how architects layer space or builders stack floors.

We move from community and connection all the way up to collaboration, communication, and vision.

Each layer feeds into the next, making the whole stronger, more intentional, and able to survive earthquakes, and stand the test of time.

Think of it as a set of interconnected, nested projects, each one adding depth to the user experience, each layer making the next one meaningful. There’s no skipping steps. Each layer grows organically from the last, turning raw community insights into a guiding vision for the future.

Community as foundation

Every meaningful product, or service, or place starts with people.

For user research, the community is our floor, the foundation for everything that comes after.

To build anything worthwhile, you need to be grounded in reality, and then move to imagination.

Communities are messy, filled with contradictions, contrasting motivations, and hidden needs.

The art is in embracing this complexity and seeing the opportunities within it.

This is where research begins, not in some clean, data-driven space but in the unpredictable real world.

Community as a foundation means deeply understanding both external dynamics (customers, prospects, markets, even competitors) and internal culture.

You dig in, become part of it, listen until you don’t just hear words but also understand the silence between them.

This immersion, this becoming a real part of the community, is where the truth lives.

This is what anthropologists call the “emic”, or the insider’s perspective.

Connection

Building bridges

After understanding the community, the next step is connection.

Taking what you know and starting to build bridges.

Connection is about starting conversations, getting people to “sit together”, and realizing they’re all part of the same story. The goal here is creating shared understanding.

You do this through workshops, through co-creation, through teaching and learning side-by-side. It’s about creating fertile ground where ideas can grow and people feel heard. We listen not to check off a task, but to build understanding (or empathy when possible) that becomes actionable.

Collaboration

turning connection into action

Now comes collaboration, where things get concrete.

Collaboration is taking connection and doing something with it: making progress.

It means working on something, and starting from common needs. This is the layer where insight turns into action, where researchers, designers, PMs, and engineers meet and see that the whole is much smarter than any individual part.

Start small. Go after tasks that both customers and teams see as important. It’s these early, manageable wins that build trust and momentum. No massive initiatives, just incremental wins. Those first collaborative victories that prove the value of working together. It’s all about building the foundation for systemic change.

Communication, keeping the pace

The next layer is communication.

Keeping everyone, from internal stakeholders to end users, in the loop about what’s happening, what’s changing, and why it matters. Communication is storytelling. It’s about saying, “Here’s what we’re learning, here’s how we’re growing, and here’s where we’re heading.”

Internally, this means preventing silos, making sure everyone knows the why behind the what. For users, it means being transparent about what’s happening, giving them visibility and ownership. It’s a story where we’re all part of the plot.

Vision

designing for the future

Finally, there’s vision.

The aspirational piece that gives meaning to all the other layers. Vision is the direction that keeps the work purposeful and focused, grounding future possibilities in the realities, mental models and insights we’ve uncovered along the way.

A solid vision asks, “What kind of future do we want to create?” and makes sure that every research activity, every decision is driving towards that picture. It’s the highest layer, but without everything below it, it’s just fantasy. Vision is what happens when community, connection, collaboration, and communication all come together to create something lasting, something bigger than any of us.

✰✰✰

In this approach, every layer matters. You don’t get to the top without building from the ground up. You don’t reach vision without community. You don’t get collaboration without connection. It’s about looking into each part of the process and allowing it to inform, transform, and elevate the next, until what you have is a structure built with intention, authenticity.
